## Design Context

### Users
Two adults (a couple) managing personal and freelance finances together. Used regularly — weekly or monthly — to upload invoices, review quarterly expenses, and prepare for tax filing. Often used at a desk reviewing numbers; occasionally on mobile to quickly snap an invoice photo. The context is focused and intentional: they open the app to get a task done, not to browse.

### Brand Personality
**Three words**: Precise. Composed. Confident.

Sharp and fintech — like a well-designed personal finance product (think Revolut or Mercury), not a generic admin panel or playful startup. Financial data deserves seriousness. The interface should feel like it takes money seriously without being cold or corporate.

Emotional goal: the user should feel **in control** and **competent** when using it.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Feel**: Polished product — something the user would be proud to show others
- **Theme**: Both light and dark mode (with toggle)
- **Anti-references**: Generic SaaS admin panels, heavy enterprise software (SAP-style), anything too casual or playful
- **References**: Revolut, Mercury Bank, Linear — sharp edges, strong typography, data-forward layouts

### Design Principles
1. **Data first** — information hierarchy over decoration; numbers and names should be easy to scan
2. **Confident minimalism** — space and silence are design choices, not omissions
3. **Precision over warmth** — sharp, clear, trustworthy; this handles financial data
4. **No unnecessary chrome** — every UI element must earn its place
5. **Light and dark equally good** — neither mode is an afterthought
